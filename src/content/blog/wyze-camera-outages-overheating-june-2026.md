# Wyze Camera Outages & Overheating During June 2026: What You Need to Know

In June 2026, Wyze issued a recall for its Solar Cam Pan (model WYZESCPWH) due to a serious fire and burn hazard caused by incorrect installation instructions leading to battery puncture and overheating. While not a widespread system outage directly linked to a heatwave, this recall addresses a critical overheating risk for a specific product. Users may also experience general connectivity issues unrelated to this recall or environmental heat.

## What happened

In June 2026, Wyze initiated a recall for its Solar Cam Pan security camera, specifically model WYZESCPWH, due to significant fire and burn hazards. The core of the problem stemmed from incorrect assembly instructions provided with the camera, which could lead users to inadvertently puncture the internal lithium-ion battery with a long flat-head screw during installation. This accidental puncture could cause the battery to rapidly overheat, posing a serious safety risk.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) reported a total of 13 incidents involving the recalled cameras overheating. Among these, six reports described units exploding or catching fire, and another six detailed minor burn injuries to users. The recall affected approximately 321,360 units sold in the U.S. and an additional 2,560 units in Canada. These cameras were sold between October 2025 and April 2026 through various retailers, including Amazon and Walmart, as well as directly from Wyze's website.

Prior to the formal recall, Wyze had already taken steps to address concerns, disabling setup support for the Solar Cam Pan in April 2026 while actively collaborating with the CPSC on a comprehensive safety review. It is important to note that this recall is distinct from a widespread system outage directly caused by a heatwave. While individual devices may experience performance issues in extreme temperatures, the Solar Cam Pan recall is a specific product defect. For context, the Wyze Cam v4, a different model, has an operating temperature range of -4°F to 122°F (-20°C to 50°C).

Separately, Wyze users reported various app and device connectivity issues in March and April 2026. Wyze acknowledged these as service disruptions unrelated to environmental factors like a heatwave and worked to resolve them. These incidents highlight the multifaceted nature of security camera reliability, encompassing both hardware safety and consistent cloud service performance.

## Impact on existing owners

![Impact on existing owners](/img/wyze-camera-outages-overheating-june-2026/inline-3.webp)


**For Wyze Solar Cam Pan (WYZESCPWH) owners:**

If you own a Wyze Solar Cam Pan, model WYZESCPWH, it is imperative to immediately stop using the recalled camera to prevent potential fire and burn hazards. You can verify your camera's model number, WYZESCPWH, which is typically located on the back of the device.

Wyze Labs has provided several options for affected customers: a free replacement camera with a solar panel accessory, a full refund for the original purchase price (which was around $80), or a gift card. It is crucial to follow Wyze's instructions for the proper disposal of the recalled lithium-ion battery-powered camera, adhering to all local and state regulations for electronic waste.

Wyze can be reached toll-free at 888-832-7226, via email at recall@wyze.com, or through their dedicated recall page at wyze.com/SCPrecall.

**For owners of other Wyze cameras experiencing offline issues:**

If you own a different Wyze camera model and are experiencing offline issues, unrelated to the Solar Cam Pan recall, you should attempt basic troubleshooting steps. These include power cycling the camera by unplugging it and plugging it back in, and restarting your home router. It's important to note that remote troubleshooting for devices that appear offline is often not possible.

Always check the official Wyze service status page (support.wyze.com/servicestatus) for any ongoing widespread outages or known issues affecting the Wyze app or services. Ensuring your camera's firmware is up to date is also a critical step, as updates frequently include stability and connectivity improvements.

If your camera has a microSD card installed and local recording enabled, it may still be recording footage even if it appears offline in the app or if cloud services are experiencing disruptions. This highlights the benefit of local storage as a backup.

### Decision Framework: Choosing Your Security Camera

To navigate the crowded security camera market, consider your specific needs and environment:

*   **If you're a renter concerned about easy installation and portability:** Prioritize battery-powered cameras with magnetic mounts and local storage options to avoid drilling and minimize reliance on cloud services. Look for models with good Wi-Fi range.
*   **If you're a homeowner seeking continuous surveillance for key areas:** Prioritize wired cameras for consistent power and reliable connectivity. Look for models with 24/7 continuous recording capabilities to a local microSD card or Network Video Recorder (NVR), and consider robust weatherproofing for outdoor use.
*   **If you own a small business requiring detailed monitoring and archival:** Prioritize high-resolution cameras (2K or 4K) with ample local storage (large capacity microSD or NVR support) and advanced motion detection features. Evaluate professional installation options and consider systems with multiple camera management capabilities.
*   **If you have unreliable Wi-Fi or no internet at your monitoring location:** Prioritize cameras with strong standalone local recording to microSD cards and the ability to operate without a constant internet connection. Some cellular-enabled cameras might also be an option, though they come with their own data plan costs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What should I do if my Wyze camera is offline but not the recalled Solar Cam Pan?

If your Wyze camera, other than the Solar Cam Pan (WYZESCPWH), is offline, first try basic troubleshooting like power cycling the camera and restarting your Wi-Fi router. Then, check the official Wyze service status page (support.wyze.com/servicestatus) for any reported outages or known issues. Ensure your camera's firmware is up to date, as this can often resolve connectivity problems.

### Can extreme heat cause my Wyze camera to stop working or go offline?

Yes, extreme temperatures can affect the performance of electronics, including security cameras. Most Wyze cameras have specified operating temperature ranges; for example, the Wyze Cam v4 operates between -4°F and 122°F (-20°C to 50°C). Exceeding these limits, especially in direct sunlight during a heatwave, could lead to overheating, temporary shutdowns, or reduced functionality.

### Is my Wyze camera still recording if it's offline in the app?

If your Wyze camera has a microSD card installed and local recording is enabled (either continuous or event-based), it should continue to record footage to the card even if it loses its internet connection or appears offline in the Wyze app. You would need to physically remove the microSD card and view the footage on a computer to access these recordings.

### How does Wyze handle my privacy and data security?

Wyze states they do not sell user data for money. They use AES 256-bit encryption for data in transit and at rest to protect your information. However, like many online services, they may share certain data for purposes like interest-based advertisements. It's always recommended to review their current privacy policy on wyze.com for the most up-to-date information.

### What is the difference between local and cloud storage for Wyze cameras?

Local storage, typically via a microSD card inserted directly into the camera, allows your camera to record video footage directly onto the card without needing an internet connection or a subscription. Cloud storage, often part of a Wyze Cam Plus subscription, uploads video recordings to Wyze's servers over the internet, allowing you to access them remotely via the app. Local storage provides a backup if internet or cloud services fail, while cloud storage offers remote access and often more advanced features.
