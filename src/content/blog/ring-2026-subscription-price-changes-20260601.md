For homeowners, this means navigating new names, higher entry costs for professional monitoring, and the long-awaited arrival of continuous recording.

# Ring 2026 Subscription Price Changes: The Full Cost of Staying in the Ecosystem

In 2026, Ring subscriptions (now Solo, Multi, and Pro) range from around $5/month to $20/month. The top tier now includes 24/7 continuous recording for eligible wired cameras, while legacy users are being moved to current market rates.

## What happened

In the first quarter of 2026, Ring officially retired the "Ring Home" branding, replacing it with a simplified but more expensive three-tier structure: Solo, Multi, and Pro. This change isn't just a naming exercise; it represents a fundamental shift in how Ring monetizes its hardware. While the brand has historically focused on event-based recording, the 2026 update centers on "synchronous utility"—features that allow the camera to act more like a communication device and a traditional 24/7 security system.

### The 2026 Rebrand: From 'Home' to 'Solo/Multi/Pro'

The new tiers are designed to segment users based on device density and desired interactivity. The **Ring Solo** plan, priced around $5/month or $50/year, is the successor to the Basic plan. It remains tethered to a single device. The **Ring Multi** plan, at approximately $10/month or $100/year, covers unlimited devices at a single location. 

The most significant addition is the **Ring Pro** plan. At around $20/month or $200/year, it is now the mandatory tier for anyone requiring professional alarm monitoring or the new 24/7 Continuous Video Recording (CVR) feature. This consolidation effectively doubles the annual cost for users who previously relied on the older $100/year "Protect Plus" monitoring rates.

### New Feature: 24/7 Continuous Recording Requirements

For years, Ring users have requested continuous recording to match competitors like Nest and Reolink. In 2026, Ring finally delivered, but with strict hardware and subscription gatekeeping. CVR is only available on the **Ring Pro** plan and is restricted to "eligible" wired or plug-in devices, such as the Stick Up Cam Pro and the Video Doorbell Pro 2 (<a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=ring.com&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">ring.com</a>). 

According to <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Ring%E2%80%99s+updated+technical+specifications&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Ring’s updated technical specifications</a>, the CVR feature provides a 14-day rolling window of continuous footage. This is a cloud-only feature; even with a Ring Alarm Pro and an SD card, the 24/7 stream is processed and stored in Ring’s data centers, necessitating a high-bandwidth upload connection.

### The End of the Professional Monitoring Add-on

Previously, professional monitoring was an available add-on or included in mid-tier plans. As of 2026, Ring has moved professional monitoring exclusively into the $200/year Pro tier. This "all-or-nothing" approach simplifies the marketing but forces a price hike on users who only wanted alarm monitoring without the advanced video features like CVR or "Doorbell Calls."

## Why it matters for buyers

The 2026 price changes fundamentally alter the value proposition of the Ring ecosystem. For new buyers, the "sticker price" of a camera is now less relevant than the long-term service fees required to make that camera useful. When you factor in the requirement for the Pro tier to access 24/7 recording, the Ring ecosystem becomes one of the most expensive consumer options on the market.

### The Hidden Cost of 24/7 Recording Hardware

While the Pro subscription enables 24/7 recording, the hardware requirements add a secondary layer of cost. Battery-powered cameras—the bulk of Ring’s historical sales—are ineligible for CVR due to power consumption and thermal management constraints (pcmag.com). Buyers looking for 24/7 coverage must invest in "Pro" or "Plus" wired models, which typically sit in the $150–$250 price band. 

Furthermore, 24/7 cloud recording requires significant upstream bandwidth. Users with multiple cameras on the Pro plan may find they need to upgrade their ISP upload speeds, adding another "hidden" monthly cost to the system's operation.

### Privacy and the Axon 'Community Requests' Partnership

A pivotal change in 2026 is the formalization of Ring’s partnership with Axon (the makers of TASER and police body cams). Ring has transitioned away from the controversial "Request for Assistance" (RFA) tool in the Neighbors app, replacing it with a streamlined "Community Requests" portal powered by Axon Evidence (pbs.org). 

Under this new framework, law enforcement requests for footage are handled through a more formalized digital evidence management system. While Ring maintains that users must still consent to share footage, the deep integration with Axon’s professional police tools has raised concerns among privacy advocates regarding the "normalization" of surveillance data sharing.

### Subscription Fatigue: How Ring Compares to Nest and Arlo in 2026

Ring’s move to a $200/year flagship plan brings it in line with Arlo’s "Safe & Secure Pro" pricing but pushes it above Google Nest’s standard "Nest Aware Plus" ($150/year).

## Impact on existing owners

If you already own a Ring system, the 2026 changes are likely already affecting your billing statement. Ring has aggressively moved to eliminate "grandfathered" plans that allowed early adopters to keep lower rates for professional monitoring.

### The 'Grandfather' Clause is Dead

As of the Q1 2026 billing cycle, the legacy Protect Plus plan (the $100/year plan that included monitoring) has been officially retired. Users who were paying $100/year for professional monitoring are being migrated to the $200/year Ring Pro plan. While Ring offered a one-time "loyalty credit" for some users during the transition, the baseline cost for a monitored Ring Alarm system has now effectively doubled (<a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=reddit.com%2Fr%2FRing&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">reddit.com/r/Ring</a>).

### What Happens to Your Existing Video History?

The migration to Solo, Multi, and Pro does not delete your existing event-based video history, provided there was no lapse in coverage. However, the new 14-day CVR window only begins accumulating data once you upgrade to the Pro plan and enable the feature on a per-camera basis. It is important to note that switching from a Multi plan to a Solo plan (downsizing) will result in the loss of video history for all cameras except the one designated for the Solo plan.

### Forced Migrations: How to Check Your New Billing Rate

Users should check their "Plan Summary" in the Ring app or via <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=ring.com&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">ring.com</a>. If you see "Ring Pro" listed and you do not use professional monitoring or 24/7 recording, you may be overpaying. Many users were automatically migrated to the Pro tier if they had a Ring Alarm system, even if they weren't utilizing all the new video features.

## What to do now

The shift in Ring's pricing model requires a strategic audit of your home security setup. Blindly staying on the "default" plan could cost you an extra $100 per year for features you don't use.

### Step 1: Audit Your Current Subscription

The first step is a device count. If you only have two cameras, paying for the "Multi" plan ($100/year) is technically identical in cost to paying for two "Solo" plans ($50 x 2). However, the Multi plan includes "Doorbell Calls"—a feature that allows you to answer your doorbell like a FaceTime call rather than a standard notification ([cnet.com](https://www.cnet.com)). If you have three or more cameras, the Multi plan is the mathematical breaking point for value.

### Step 2: Optimize Your Plan for Your Device Count

If you are currently on the $200/year Pro plan solely for professional monitoring but don't care about 24/7 recording, you are in a difficult position. Ring has effectively tied these two disparate features together. 

**Decision Framework: Which Tier Fits You?**

*   **The Renter (1 Doorbell):** Stick with **Ring Solo**. The extra $5/month for Multi offers no benefit for a single-device setup.
*   **The Standard Homeowner (3-5 Cameras):** The **Ring Multi** plan is the "sweet spot." It covers all devices and adds the interactive Doorbell Calls feature without the $200/year sting of the Pro tier.
*   **The High-Security User:** If you require 24/7 "gapless" footage and professional monitoring, the **Ring Pro** tier is your only option within the ecosystem.
*   **The Budget-Conscious:** If the $100–$200 annual fee is a dealbreaker, it is time to look at an "exit strategy" toward local storage.

### Step 3: Exploring Local Storage Alternatives (The 'No-Fee' Route)

If the 2026 price hikes have pushed you past your limit, several competitors offer 24/7 recording with zero monthly fees. Brands like Reolink and Eufy have gained significant market share by offering "local-first" storage.

Ultimately, Ring’s 2026 subscription changes reinforce the brand’s position as a premium service provider. You aren't just buying a camera; you are leasing a security service. For those who value the seamless integration of Amazon Alexa and Ring Alarm, the $200/year Pro tier may be a justifiable expense. For everyone else, the best no-subscription security cameras are looking more attractive than ever.
