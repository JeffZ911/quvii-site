# Google Nest Community Forum Deletion June 30: Save Your Data Now

**Quick Answer:** On June 30, 2026, Google will permanently delete the legacy [Nest Community](https://quvii.com/blog/google-nest-community-migration-outage-fixes-2026-20260625) forum. No user posts, guides, or account history will be migrated to the new platform; users must manually archive important troubleshooting data before the deadline.

## What happened

In May 2026, Google surprised the smart home community by announcing the complete sunsetting of its long-standing community platform, `googlenestcommunity.com`. This site, which has served as the primary peer-to-peer support hub for Nest products since the brand's acquisition, is being replaced by a streamlined, internal domain at `support.google.com/googlenest/community`.

The transition is not a migration in the traditional sense. While most tech companies port historical data to new platforms to maintain a "knowledge base," Google has confirmed that no user-generated content will be moved. On June 30, 2026, the old servers will be wiped. This includes over a decade of technical deep-dives, custom integration workarounds for "Works with Nest" legacy systems, and step-by-step resolution guides for hardware that Google no longer officially supports in its primary documentation.

### The 'Snip Snap' Migration Strategy
Industry analysts have dubbed this the "Snip Snap" strategy—a clean break from the past. By choosing not to migrate data, Google effectively resets the community's collective memory. For the user, this means that a search for a specific Nest Cam IQ sensor failure from 2021 will no longer yield the 50-page community thread that solved it. Instead, users will be greeted by the new platform's "day zero" state, which prioritizes current-generation hardware and Google’s new AI-driven support tools.

### Why Google is Switching Platforms
The move is primarily driven by Google’s broader initiative to consolidate all product support under a single infrastructure. The legacy forum was built on a third-party platform (Khoros), which required separate account management and data silos. By moving to the `support.google.com` domain, Google can:
1.  **Integrate Gemini for Home:** The new forum is built to feed Google’s LLM (Large Language Model), allowing the "Gemini for Home" assistant to scan community posts in real-time to provide automated answers.
2.  **Unified Identity:** Users no longer need a separate "Community Profile." Support interactions are now tied directly to the primary Google Account.
3.  **Moderation Efficiency:** Centralizing support allows Google’s internal teams to moderate content more aggressively, ensuring that official "Recommended Answers" are not buried under community-driven workarounds that might bypass Google’s preferred ecosystem settings.

## Why it matters for buyers

![Why it matters for buyers](/img/google-nest-community-forum-deletion-june-30/inline-2.webp)


For those currently shopping for security cameras, the deletion of the Nest forum is more than a technical footnote; it is a shift in how the brand handles transparency. When you buy into a camera ecosystem, you are also buying into its support lifecycle.

### Loss of Historical Reliability Data
Prospective buyers often use community forums to gauge the long-term reliability of a product. A "4.5-star" review on a retail site often misses the fact that, six months later, a firmware update might have caused a 2-second lag in person detection. The legacy Nest forum was a repository for these real-world reports. By deleting this history, Google is effectively removing the "paper trail" of past hardware flaws, such as the peeling "anti-reflective" coating on older Nest Outdoor cams or the battery-drain issues that plagued early Nest Doorbell (Battery) units in cold climates.

### The Shift Toward Controlled Support Narratives
The new platform is designed to be "solution-oriented," which in corporate terms often means prioritizing official fixes over community-discovered ones. If a community member finds a way to integrate a Nest camera into a third-party dashboard that Google doesn't officially support, that information is less likely to survive or be promoted on the new platform. This creates a "knowledge gap" where the only available information is what Google has vetted.

## Impact on existing owners

![Impact on existing owners](/img/google-nest-community-forum-deletion-june-30/inline-3.webp)


If you currently have Nest cameras installed, the June 30 deadline is a ticking clock. The most immediate impact is the loss of specific technical fixes for legacy hardware that Google no longer prioritizes in its help center.

### Legacy Hardware Vulnerability
Owners of the original **Nest Hello (Wired)** and **Nest Cam IQ** are particularly at risk. These devices often face specific error codes that official documentation glosses over. For example, the **NC023 error** (a Bluetooth pairing failure) has dozens of community-contributed "deep resets" that involve specific timing of the reset button and app cache clearing. Once the forum is deleted, these community-verified "hacks" will be gone.

**Common Nest Error Codes with Community-Only Fixes:**
*   **C107 / C119:** Wi-Fi chip handshake failures often solved by specific router channel adjustments found in forum threads.
*   **NC023:** Bluetooth pairing loops during setup.
*   **C327:** Power delivery issues on older Nest Hello units related to internal battery degradation.
*   **N260:** "Mismatched voltage" errors that community members solved using specific 24V transformer brands.

### The Loss of Community Experts
The legacy forum was home to "Gold Product Experts"—volunteers who spent thousands of hours helping others. These users are not being "ported" over with their history. While they can create new accounts, their years of established credibility, badges, and—most importantly—their library of past solutions will be erased. Many experts have expressed frustration on Reddit (r/Nest), suggesting they may not return to the new platform, leading to a "brain drain" of Nest technical knowledge.

### Gemini for Home 3.1 and the Notification Bug
Recent threads on the legacy forum have been dominated by the "Gemini for Home 3.1" update, which integrated more AI features into the Google Home app. However, users have reported a significant bug where person-detection notifications are delayed by up to 30 seconds. On the legacy forum, users have been collaborating on "roll-back" strategies and cache-clearing fixes. When June 30 hits, this collective evidence of a systemic software bug will be deleted, making it harder for users to hold Google accountable for a fix.

## What to do now

![What to do now](/img/google-nest-community-forum-deletion-june-30/inline-4.webp)


You have until June 30, 2026, to secure the information you need to keep your home security system running. Do not assume that a simple Google search will find these answers on July 1.

### How to Archive Your Threads
If there is a specific thread that helped you fix your camera in the past, or a guide on how to integrate your Nest cams with a third-party NVR, archive it now.
1.  **Print to PDF:** Open the thread, expand all replies, and use `Ctrl+P` (or `Cmd+P`) to save the entire page as a PDF. Store this in your "Home Maintenance" folder on Google Drive or iCloud.
2.  **Wayback Machine:** Copy the URL of important threads and paste them into the Internet Archive’s Wayback Machine. This ensures the global community can still access the data after Google deletes the live site.
3.  **Screenshot Key Diagrams:** Many community fixes include wiring diagrams for doorbells or internal teardown photos of cameras. These are rarely captured well by PDF converters; take manual screenshots of these images.

### Where the Nest Community is Moving
While Google wants users to move to `support.google.com/googlenest/community`, the "uncensored" technical discussion is migrating elsewhere:
*   **r/Nest and r/GoogleHome:** These Reddit communities are now the primary hubs for technical deep-dives. Unlike the official forum, these are not controlled by Google and will maintain their history indefinitely.
*   **Discord:** Several "Smart Home" Discord servers have created dedicated Nest channels to capture the influx of users fleeing the forum deletion.
*   **Google Takeout:** You can try to export your own data via Google Takeout, but be warned: historically, Takeout has been inconsistent in capturing forum posts made on third-party platforms like Khoros. It may only export your profile metadata, not the actual content of your helpful posts.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will my Google Nest account be deleted?
No, your Google Account and your access to the Google Home app will remain unchanged. Only the data on the community forum website (`googlenestcommunity.com`) is being deleted. Your camera footage, subscription status, and device settings are not affected by this migration.

### Can I still get help for my older Nest Cam IQ or Nest Hello?
You can still post questions on the new Google Support forum, but you will no longer be able to search the decade-old archive of existing solutions. Official Google support for legacy hardware is often limited to "factory reset" suggestions, so losing the community-driven "deep fixes" is a significant loss for owners of older devices.

### Why didn't Google just move the posts to the new site?
Google has cited "data privacy and platform compatibility" as the reasons for not migrating content. Because the old forum was hosted by a third-party vendor (Khoros), moving thousands of user-generated posts and personal profiles into Google’s internal support infrastructure presented legal and technical hurdles that the company chose not to clear.
