On June 10, 2026, Botslab (the smart home security arm of 360 Smart Life) officially released the W101 Window Camera to the North American and European markets. The device arrives shortly after receiving a Red Dot Design Award, a distinction largely earned for its unique "sandwich" form factor that allows it to cling to the interior of a window while providing a 360-degree-style awareness through two distinct lenses.

The headline feature is "Dual-View" technology. Unlike previous window cameras that focused solely on the street, the W101 houses two independent 2.5K (4-megapixel) sensors. The "OutView" lens monitors the exterior through the glass, while the "InView" lens covers the interior room. This effectively turns a single window pane into a comprehensive security hub for entry points like front porches or balcony sliders.

Technically, the W101 addresses the primary failure point of window cameras: [night vision](https://quvii.com/learn/unifi-camera-night-vision-stuck-daytime-fix). Standard cameras use Infrared (IR) LEDs that reflect off glass, blinding the sensor at night. Botslab has bypassed this by utilizing an F1.0 large-aperture lens system. By allowing significantly more light into the sensor, the camera can maintain full-color imaging in low-light conditions without the need for reflective IR illumination.

Positioned as a "no-drill" solution, the W101 is aimed squarely at renters and apartment dwellers who are often prohibited from mounting hardware to exterior siding or masonry. The unit is currently priced in the under $100 range for single units, with multi-pack bundles available for those seeking whole-home coverage.

### The Dual-Lens Innovation: OutView vs. InView
The W101 is the first consumer-grade window camera to successfully integrate two high-resolution sensors into a chassis small enough to avoid obstructing the user's view. According to Botslab's official product documentation, the OutView lens features a wide-angle field of view optimized for porch monitoring, while the InView lens is designed to capture the immediate interior perimeter. This configuration is particularly useful for monitoring package deliveries and verifying if a door was left ajar from the inside, all from a single app feed.

### Solving the Glare Problem: The F1.0 Optical Breakthrough
The shift to an F1.0 aperture is the most critical technical upgrade in the W101. In photography and surveillance, a lower f-number indicates a wider lens opening. Most consumer cameras hover between F1.6 and F2.0. By moving to F1.0, the W101 collects roughly 2.5 times more light than standard sensors. This allows the camera to rely on ambient streetlights or porch lights to produce a color image at night, completely eliminating the need for IR LEDs that typically cause "white-out" reflections when placed against glass (PRNewswire).

### Tool-Free Installation: The Electrostatic Sticker Method
Traditional window cameras rely on VHB (Very High Bond) adhesives that can be difficult to remove or leave permanent residue. The W101 utilizes an electrostatic sticker—similar to those used for dash cams—to adhere the mounting bracket to the glass. This method relies on atmospheric pressure and static charge rather than chemical glues, making it an ideal no-drill security camera solution. The bracket also features a cable management channel to keep the USB-C power run flush against the window frame.

## Why it matters for buyers

For the average consumer, the W101 represents a consolidation of hardware. Previously, securing an apartment entry required an outdoor camera (often banned by HOAs) and an [indoor camera](https://quvii.com/learn/arlo-essential-indoor-camera-blinking-blue-loop). By combining these into a single window-mounted unit, buyers reduce their initial hardware investment and simplify their network load.

The inclusion of dual-band Wi-Fi (2.4GHz and 5GHz) is equally significant. In high-density apartment buildings, the 2.4GHz spectrum is often saturated, leading to dropped frames and lagging notifications. The W101’s ability to utilize the 5GHz band ensures that the high-bandwidth 2.5K dual streams remain stable, providing a smoother "[live view](https://quvii.com/learn/tapo-camera-live-view-streaming-failure-fix-20260620)" experience.

### The Renter's Edge: Security Without Property Damage
Renters face a unique challenge: they need security but cannot modify the building. The W101 addresses this by remaining entirely inside the thermal envelope of the home. Because it doesn't require drilling through exterior walls for power or mounting, it circumvents most lease restrictions. This makes it a top contender for those seeking window security cameras that can be removed in seconds without leaving a trace.

### Local Storage vs. Cloud: Breaking the Subscription Cycle
In an era where brands like Ring and Arlo are increasingly locking basic features behind monthly paywalls, Botslab has maintained a no-subscription security camera option. The W101 supports local recording to a microSD card (up to 256GB). Using H.265 (HEVC) video compression, the device can store weeks of motion events or several days of continuous 2.5K footage without requiring a cloud plan.

With an "InView" lens constantly facing the living area, privacy is a valid concern. ### Pre-Installation Checklist: Glass and Signal Prep
1.  **Clean the Glass:** Use a 70% isopropyl alcohol solution to clean the interior mounting area. Any oils or dust will cause the electrostatic sticker to fail over time.
2.  **Test Wi-Fi Strength:** Place your phone against the window glass where the camera will sit. If you cannot get at least two bars of 5GHz Wi-Fi, consider a mesh node nearby; 2.5K dual-streams are sensitive to signal jitter.
3.  **Check for Obstructions:** Ensure the window screen is on the *outside* of the glass. If your screen is between the camera and the street, it will create a "moiré effect" (a mesh pattern) on the video.

### Selecting the Right MicroSD for 24/7 Recording
Because the W101 records two streams simultaneously, the write-load on the microSD card is doubled compared to standard cameras. We recommend "High Endurance" cards specifically rated for surveillance or dash cam use (e.g., SanDisk High Endurance or Samsung PRO Endurance).

*   **128GB Card:** Provides approximately 4–6 days of continuous dual 2.5K recording.
*   **256GB Card:** Provides approximately 9–12 days of continuous dual 2.5K recording.

### Is the W101 Right for Your Home Layout?
The W101 is most effective when placed on a window that is parallel to a walkway or entry path. Because the camera sits flat against the glass, its "lateral" view is limited compared to a camera mounted on a swivel arm. If your window is recessed deeply into a wall, the "OutView" lens may have a narrowed field of vision. In such cases, the Botslab W510—which offers more articulation—might be a better, albeit more invasive, alternative.

### Frequently Asked Questions

### Does the Botslab W101 work with double-pane or triple-pane windows?
Yes, the F1.0 lens system is designed to handle the slight internal reflections inherent in multi-pane glass. However, users may notice a slight "ghosting" effect on bright light sources (like streetlamps) due to the light bouncing between the panes. This is a physical limitation of glass, not the camera sensor.

### Can I turn off the internal camera if I only want to see outside?
Yes. The Botslab app allows you to independently toggle the "InView" and "OutView" lenses. You can also use the physical privacy slider to mechanically block the internal lens while leaving the external lens active for security.

### What happens if the power goes out?
The W101 is a wired camera and does not contain an internal battery. If power is lost, recording will stop. For critical security, we recommend plugging the camera’s USB-C adapter into a small Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) to maintain coverage during short outages.

### Will the electrostatic mount fall off in extreme heat or cold?
Electrostatic mounts are generally more stable than suction cups in extreme temperatures because they do not rely on a rubber seal that can harden or crack. However, in climates with extreme direct sunlight (over 100°F), the glass temperature can exceed the operating range of the camera itself. Ensure your window has some shade or is double-paned to insulate the device.

### Does the W101 support ONVIF or RTSP for third-party NVRs?
As of the current firmware (v2026.6), Botslab has not enabled official ONVIF support for the W101. The device is intended to be used within the Botslab app ecosystem or via official integrations with Alexa and Google Home.
