# Ring Battery Doorbell Plus Firmware Update: What You Need to Know in June 2026

As of June 2026, Ring has not announced a specific firmware update for the [Ring Battery Doorbell Plus](/learn/ring-battery-doorbell-plus-motion-zone-issues) with a release date of June 2026. However, Ring devices, including the Battery Doorbell Plus, regularly receive automatic firmware updates to enhance security, improve performance, and introduce new features, which users can monitor via the Ring app's Device Health section. It's important to distinguish this from the release of the Ring Doorbell Plus (2nd Generation) around June 2026, which is a new hardware model rather than a firmware update for the existing device.

Firmware serves as the operational foundation for your security hardware. Unlike the Ring app on your smartphone, which handles the user interface, firmware is the "embedded" software living on the doorbell's internal chip. It dictates how the camera sensor processes light, how the Wi-Fi radio maintains a handshake with your router, and how the motion sensors distinguish between a swaying tree branch and a delivery person.

### Understanding Firmware for Your Video Doorbell
For the Ring Battery Doorbell Plus, firmware updates are the primary vehicle for maintaining "feature parity" with newer models. While the physical camera lens cannot be upgraded via software, the algorithms that interpret the image can be. For instance, Ring has historically used firmware updates to refine its "Head-to-Toe" video perspective and improve the accuracy of its 1536p resolution processing <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=ring.com%2Fsupport&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">ring.com/support</a>. In June 2026, firmware remains the mechanism that ensures the original Battery Doorbell Plus stays compatible with the latest versions of the Ring app and the broader Amazon Alexa ecosystem.

### Distinguishing Firmware Updates from New Product Releases
A common point of confusion for consumers in mid-2026 is the distinction between software maintenance and hardware iterations. The "Ring Doorbell Plus (2nd Generation)," which debuted around June 2026, features physical hardware changes—potentially including upgraded Wi-Fi 6 radios or enhanced battery chemistry. Owners of the original Battery Doorbell Plus will continue to receive firmware updates to keep their devices secure, but these updates cannot "download" the physical capabilities of the 2nd Gen hardware. Firmware optimizes what you already own; it does not transform the physical device into a newer model.

## Why it exists

![Why it exists](/img/ring-battery-doorbell-plus-firmware-update-june-2026/inline-2.webp)


The necessity of firmware updates in the security camera industry stems from the "cat-and-mouse" nature of cybersecurity and the ongoing evolution of smart home standards.

### Boosting Security and Privacy
The most critical reason for any firmware update is the deployment of security patches. As researchers identify new vulnerabilities in IoT (Internet of Things) devices, manufacturers must "patch" these holes to prevent unauthorized access. In 2026, with the increasing sophistication of network-based attacks, these updates often include refreshed encryption certificates and strengthened protocols for end-to-end encryption. Ring’s privacy documentation emphasizes that keeping firmware current is a prerequisite for maintaining the integrity of their "Video End-to-End Encryption" feature <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=ring.com%2Fprivacy&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">ring.com/privacy</a>.

### Enhancing Performance and Reliability
Firmware updates often address "under-the-hood" operational efficiencies. For a battery-powered device like the Doorbell Plus, this frequently involves:
*   **Power Management:** Optimizing how the device sleeps and wakes up to extend the interval between charges.
*   **Connectivity Stability:** Adjusting how the device handles "RSSI" (Received Signal Strength Indicator) fluctuations to prevent the doorbell from dropping off the Wi-Fi network.
*   **Motion Accuracy:** Refining the "Package Detection" and "Person Detection" algorithms to reduce false-positive alerts, which in turn saves battery life by reducing unnecessary recordings.

### Introducing New Features
Occasionally, Ring uses firmware to unlock capabilities that were not present at launch. This might include new "Quick Replies" (the automated voice responses the doorbell can give) or improved "Color Pre-Roll" processing, which provides a few seconds of video from before a motion event was triggered. By June 2026, firmware updates have also been used to ensure Matter compatibility, allowing the doorbell to interact more fluidly with non-Amazon smart home hubs.

## How it works under the hood

![How it works under the hood](/img/ring-battery-doorbell-plus-firmware-update-june-2026/inline-3.webp)


The update process for a Ring device is designed to be invisible to the user, but it relies on several technical prerequisites to succeed.

### Automatic Delivery and Installation
Ring utilizes an "Over-the-Air" (OTA) update system. When the Ring servers flag a device for an update, the doorbell downloads the software package during a period of inactivity. For the Battery Doorbell Plus, the device must have a minimum battery charge—typically above 30%—to initiate the installation. If the battery is too low, the update will be deferred until the device is recharged. This safeguard prevents the device from "bricking" (becoming non-functional) due to a power loss during the sensitive installation phase.

### Monitoring Updates via the Ring App
Users can verify their current status by navigating to:
**Devices > [Your Doorbell] > Device Health.**
Under the "Device Details" section, the "Firmware" field will either display a version number or state "Up to Date." If an update is currently in progress, you may see a message stating "Updating" alongside a progress bar. During this time, the light ring on the front of the doorbell may flash or spin, indicating that the internal processor is writing new data to its memory.

### Technical Requirements for a Smooth Update
A successful update requires a stable 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi connection. While some newer Ring models support 5 GHz, the original Battery Doorbell Plus relies heavily on the 2.4 GHz band for its superior range through exterior walls. 
*   **RSSI Values:** For firmware to download without corruption, an RSSI between -40 and -60 is ideal. If the RSSI is worse than -70, the update may fail repeatedly <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=ring.com%2Fsupport&tag=jeffzen911-20" rel="sponsored nofollow noopener" target="_blank">ring.com/support</a>.
*   **Installation Time:** A standard firmware update typically takes between 5 and 15 minutes. During this window, the doorbell is effectively "offline."

## Common misconceptions

![Common misconceptions](/img/ring-battery-doorbell-plus-firmware-update-june-2026/inline-5.webp)


There are several myths regarding how Ring manages its software ecosystem that can lead to user frustration.

### The Myth of Manual Updates
A frequent question among users is "How do I force an update?" Unlike a smartphone OS, there is no "Check for Update" button in the Ring app that triggers an immediate download. Ring manages the rollout in "waves" to ensure their servers aren't overwhelmed and to monitor for any bugs that might appear in the new code. If your device says "Up to Date," it means it has the latest version *authorized for your specific device ID*, even if you read online that a newer version exists.

### Understanding Update Transparency
Ring is often criticized for its "vague" changelogs. While major feature releases (like the introduction of "Bird's Eye Zones") are accompanied by marketing emails and detailed support articles, minor firmware updates are often released with generic notes such as "Bug fixes and performance improvements." This lack of transparency can be frustrating for power users, but it is standard practice in the consumer camera industry to avoid revealing specific security patches that could be reverse-engineered by malicious actors.

### Hardware vs. Software Upgrades
As noted earlier, software cannot overcome hardware limitations. For example, the original Battery Doorbell Plus lacks the "3D Motion Detection" (radar) found in the "Pro" series. No amount of firmware updates in June 2026 will grant the Doorbell Plus radar capabilities. Users should be wary of third-party claims or YouTube "hacks" suggesting that firmware can unlock hidden hardware features.

### Subscription Dependence for Updates
There is a common misconception that you must have a Ring Protect subscription to receive firmware updates. This is false. **Firmware updates for security and basic functionality are free for the life of the device.** However, the *features* enabled by those updates—such as advanced AI package detection or cloud video storage—almost always require an active Ring Protect plan.

*   **If you are a Renter:** The Battery Doorbell Plus remains a top-tier choice. Its firmware updates continue to support the "Quick Release Battery" efficiency, and the lack of wiring requirements makes it the most flexible option for non-permanent dwellings.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is my Ring Doorbell stuck on "Updating Firmware"?
This usually indicates a "hang" during the download or installation phase, often caused by a weak Wi-Fi signal (RSSI) or the battery dropping below the critical threshold mid-update. To resolve this, ensure the battery is fully charged and consider moving your router closer to the door or using a Ring Chime Pro to extend the signal. If it remains stuck for more than an hour, a manual reset (holding the orange button for 15 seconds) may be required.

### Can I roll back a firmware update if I don't like the changes?
No. Ring does not provide a mechanism for users to "downgrade" firmware. This is a security measure to ensure that devices cannot be reverted to older, more vulnerable versions of the software. If a firmware update introduces a bug, the only solution is to wait for a subsequent "hotfix" update from Ring.

### Does the Ring Battery Doorbell Plus support Matter in June 2026?
Yes, through firmware updates released between 2024 and 2026, the Battery Doorbell Plus has gained basic Matter compatibility when used with a compatible Matter controller (like a newer Echo Hub). This allows the doorbell to trigger routines in other Matter-enabled smart home ecosystems, though advanced video features still typically require the Ring app.

### How do I know if a firmware update has improved my battery life?
The most reliable way is to check the "Power Settings" and "Battery Level" trends in the Ring app over a 14-day period following an update. Look for a decrease in the "discharge rate" relative to the number of motion events. If the battery is draining faster after an update, it may be due to a new feature (like increased motion sensitivity) being enabled by default.
